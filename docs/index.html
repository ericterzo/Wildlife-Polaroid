How to play

Photograph every species in your field book. Fields, forests, towns, and water each have their own animals — including villagers, and fish that only show up while you're wading.

Computer controls

  • Mouse — look around (click once first to grab the mouse)
  • W A S D or arrow keys — walk (relative to where you're looking)
  • Shift — run (fast, but loud — animals bolt sooner)
  • C — sneak (slow and quiet — get much closer)
  • Left-click — take a photo
  • Right-click — hold to zoom the lens
  • Space — open/close the photo book · ← → flip pages
  • Esc — pause menu: save, load, volume, cheats

Touch controls

  • Drag anywhere on the view to look around
  • Joystick (bottom-left) — move in any direction relative to your view; push it to the rim to run
  • Shutter (bottom-right) — take a photo
  • Book (bottom-centre) — your photo album
  • Slider (right edge) — zoom; + is up
  • Cog (top-right) — save, load, volume, cheats

Moving around the world

  • Paths connect the towns — follow them, or cut through fields and forests.
  • You can swim: just walk into a lake. The deeper you wade, the higher the water rises up your screen and the slower you move — that's when fish appear around you.
  • Running is for covering ground, not for hunting: animals hear you from far away. When stalking, walk — or better, sneak.

How to get good shots

  • Framing decides your points: put the animal in the center of the frame and make it look as big on screen as possible. Distance doesn't matter — the zoom is your best friend: magnify a far-away animal without ever spooking it. Three stars are earned, not given.
  • Face them: a head-on portrait is worth up to +45% over a shot of their backside. Handily, spooked animals turn to stare at you first.
  • Approach slowly. Shy animals freeze and stare for a moment before bolting — that stare is your best photo window. Most animals now tolerate you fairly close; a wary few (deer, herons) still need real stealth or a long lens.
  • Cats and dogs come to you — crouch down and wait. Villagers don't mind you unless you crowd them.
  • Sizes: every animal rolls a random size, teeny-tiny to beyond-real-life MASSIVE. Extremes are rare and worth much more. Tiny specimens score almost entirely on centering — occupancy is forgiven.
  • Combos: several animals in one shot = bonus points (+40% each). The closest animal gets the album page.
  • Birds in flight earn a 5× bonus. Birds perch in trees and occasionally cross the sky right above you — look up! Rarely a whole flock passes over: flight bonus AND combo in one shot.
  • Each photo takes a couple of seconds to develop — you can't shoot again until it's done, so make every shot count.
  • Only your best shot per species is kept, in the order you first found them.
  • Something ancient stirs… after a few minutes of exploring, venture far from your starting town and look for strange bone formations in the wilds. What lingers near them is very large, completely unbothered by you — and one of them is always hungry.
  • Curious what a species looks like? Menu → Cheats → Species gallery.

Cheats

  • Species gallery — a portrait of every animal in the book.
  • Easy mode — far more animals, closer to you, and bigger on average.
  • 🧟 Zombie survival — every animal turns. Your camera becomes a hatchet (same button). Don't let them get within arm's reach: 5 hits and you're done. A day passes every 30 seconds, and each day they come faster and in greater numbers. Score = days × 100 + kills × 10.
